01. VIBRATION

  • DEFINITION: The complete back-and-forth motion of an object from one extreme position, through the equilibrium point, to the other extreme, and back to the start
      Period: The time required for an object to make 1 vibration.
      Formula: T(Period S) = t (total time s) / n (amount of vibration)
  • Frequency: The number of vibrations done by an object in one period of time.
      Formula: f (frequency Hz) = n (number of vibration) / t (total time s).
  • Relationship between T and f:Inversley proportional, the longer the period, the less frequency produced

02. WAVES

  • Properties of Waves: A wave is a vibration that travels in a certain direction and transfer energy from one place to another.

      Mechanical Waves (According to Medium of Propagation):Need medium of propagation to travel (solid, liquid, gases). Ex: String, sound waves
      Electromagetic Waves (According to Medium of Propagation):Doesn't need medium of propagation to travel. Ex: Infrared, Radio waves

  • Wavefront: A wavefront represents the position of a wave at a certain moment.
  • Parts of a wave:
      Amplitude:
      The number of complete waves that pass a point per second
      The distance from the peak of one wave to the peak of an adjacent wave
      The distance from the equilibrium position of a peak plus the same distance from a Trough
      The maximum distance from the equilibrium position of any point on a wave
      Wavelength (λ): Distance between 2 identical points.
      Trough: The lowest point of a wave
      Crest:The Highest Point of a wave
      1 Wave: 1 Trough and 1 Crest
  • Wave Formula:
      Amount of Waves: x (Distance between 2 points m) = n (amount of waves) x λ (wavelength m)
      Speed of Waves
      v (spead of wave m/s) = x (distance between 2 points m) / t (time s)
      v (spead of wave m/s) = λ (wavelength m) x f (frequency Hz)
      v (spead of wave m/s) = λ (wavelength m) / T (Wave's period s)

02. SOUND

  • Nature of sound: Sound waves are longitudinal waves and can be reflected and refracted.
  • Sound Wave categories:
      • Infrasonic:under 20Hz
      Audiosonic:Between 20 - 20.000 Hz
      Ultrasonic:Over 20.000 Hz
    • Sound wave terms:
        Compression: area where particles are together close together
      .
        Refraction: area where particles are spread away
    • Reflection of sound: Occurs when sound waves bounce of a surface.
    • Refraction of sound: Occurs when sound waves change direction due to changes in medium or temperature.
    • Pitch and frequency:
        - High pitch = high frequency sound
        - Low pitch = low frequency sound
    • Loudness and amplitude:
        - Larger amplitude = louder sound
        - Smaller amplitude = softer sound
    • Speed of sound:
        - The speed of sound is how fast sound wave travels.
        - Sound needs a medium to travel(cannot travel in a vacuum)
        - Sounds travels at different speeds in different medium:
          - Fastest in solids
          - Slower in liquids
          - Slowest in gasses(air)
        - The speed of sound increases when the temperature increases
        - The speed of sound decreases when the temperature decreases
        - Loudness(amplitude) does not affect the speed of sound
        - Pitch(frequency) does not affect the speed of sound
        - The speed of sound can be measures using distance and time
